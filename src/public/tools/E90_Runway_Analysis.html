Method & Notes ▸ 07
Calculation assumptions

Reference anchors are the published example values on each POH chart:

• Takeoff (4‑12): 25°C, PA 3,966 ft, 9,800 lb, 9.5 kt HW → GR 1,830 ft, 50 ft 2,400 ft
• Accel‑Stop (6‑4): same conditions → 4,130 ft
• Single‑Engine Takeoff (6‑5): same conditions → GR 1,830 ft, 50 ft 4,260 ft
• Single‑Engine Climb Gradient (6‑6): same conditions → 3.65%
• Landing (4‑16): 15°C, PA 5,650 ft, 8,855 lb, 9.5 kt HW → GR 1,050 ft, 50 ft 2,160 ft

Distance corrections from the reference point:

• Density altitude: +7%/1,000 ft DA (takeoff), +4%/1,000 ft DA (landing)
• Weight: (W/Wref)2.2 takeoff, (W/Wref)1.9 landing
• Headwind: −0.5% per knot above reference HW
• Tailwind: +2.2% per knot (POH‑standard penalty)
• Slope: ±10%/%‑upslope takeoff, ∓5%/%‑upslope landing

SE climb gradient model:

Gradient (%) = 3.65 − 0.4 × (DA − 6,109)/1000 − 1.07 × (W − 9,800)/1000
Anchored to POH 6‑6 example; validated against 10,000 ft PA / ISA / 9,800 lb ≈ 2%.

Obstacle extension: If obstacle height > 50 ft, adds (h−50)/(SE gradient) horizontal distance beyond the 50 ft POH value. Conservative because SE gradient is less than two-engine climb.

Surface corrections (per FAA AC 91‑79 & common operator practice):

• Dry grass: takeoff ×1.15, landing ×1.15
• Wet paved: landing ground roll ×1.3
• Wet grass: takeoff ×1.25, landing ×1.35

V-speed scaling: VR and V50 scale with √(W/10,100). Vmca and Vyse are limit speeds and do not scale with weight in the POH.

Not modeled: contamination (snow/slush/standing water), anti‑skid inoperative, reverser-inoperative landing, landing with prop reversing, obstacle horizontal distance less than runway length, runway-intersection takeoffs. Consult the POH for these.

Disclaimer This tool produces estimates only. It is NOT FAA‑approved performance data and NOT a substitute for the FAA Approved Airplane Flight Manual (P/N 90‑590012‑3) or the Pilot's Operating Manual. Chart digitization from a scanned POH introduces unavoidable uncertainty. The pilot‑in‑command is solely responsible for verifying performance numbers against the approved POH before every takeoff and landing. Surface, slope, contamination, anti‑ice bleed loss, and obstacle assessment beyond 50 ft are not modeled. Use at your own risk.
